GENERAL INSTITUTIONAL RULES, REGULATIONS, AND PROSPECTUS

​1. ADMISSION AND REGISTRATION POLICIES ​1.1. Accuracy of Information: By submitting an application through the digital portal, the applicant certifies that all information provided—including personal details, matriculation results, and post-schooling qualifications—is true and correct. The Institution reserves the right to cancel any application or subsequent registration if it is discovered that the applicant provided fraudulent documentation or "False Info." ​1.2. Admission Point Score (APS) and Selection: Admission is subject to the applicant meeting the minimum APS requirements for their specific programme of choice. Meeting the minimum requirements does not guarantee placement, as admissions are competitive and subject to enrollment targets set by the University Senate. ​1.3. Document Vault Compliance: Applicants must upload clear, legible, and certified copies of all required documents to the Document Vault. Failure to provide documents flagged as "Missing Info" within 14 business days of notification will result in the automatic withdrawal of the application. ​2. DISCIPLINARY RULES AND STUDENT CONDUCT ​2.1. General Conduct: Every student shall at all times maintain a standard of conduct that does not bring the Institution into disrepute. This includes interactions within the digital Student Portal and physical campus grounds. ​2.2. Academic Integrity: Plagiarism, cheating during examinations, and the falsification of academic records (including the manipulation of the portal’s subject entry system) constitute gross misconduct. ​2.3. Disciplinary Procedures: The Institution may initiate disciplinary action against a student for: ​Any act of dishonesty in relation to the application process. ​Misuse of Institutional property, including the digital infrastructure and student portals. ​Unauthorized access to or interference with the University’s database systems. ​2.4. Sanctions: The University Disciplinary Committee (UDC) may impose sanctions ranging from a written warning and the forfeiture of credits to immediate expulsion and a permanent ban from future registration at the Institution. ​3. FINANCIAL POLICIES AND FEES ​3.1. Liability for Fees: A student becomes liable for the full annual tuition and residence fees upon registration. The "Paid" status on the portal must be reflected before a student is granted access to academic materials or examination results. ​3.2. Application and Registration Fees: The application fee is non-refundable, regardless of whether the applicant is accepted, rejected, or placed on a "Waiting List." Registration is only finalized upon receipt of the minimum initial payment as determined by the Finance Department. ​3.3. Payment Methods: Payments must be made via the approved digital payment gateways (e.g., Paystack) or direct bank deposit using the uniquely generated Student Number or Application ID as a reference. Use of incorrect references may result in a "Payment Status: Pending" delay for which the Institution is not liable. ​3.4. Outstanding Debt: Students with outstanding balances from previous academic cycles will be blocked from registering for the current cycle. No degree, diploma, or official transcript will be issued to any student whose account is in arrears. ​4. DATA PRIVACY AND PROTECTION (POPIA COMPLIANCE) ​4.1. Consent to Process Information: By utilizing the portal, the student consents to the Institution collecting and processing their personal, academic, and demographic data for the purposes of: ​Academic administration and placement. ​Communication regarding application status (e.g., "Under Review," "Accepted"). ​Financial auditing and fee collection. ​4.2. Third-Party Verification: The Institution reserves the right to verify matriculation results with the Department of Basic Education (DBE) and previous qualifications with the relevant statutory bodies or external institutions. ​5. RESIDENCE AND ACCOMMODATION ​5.1. Eligibility: Acceptance into an academic programme does not guarantee placement in University-managed housing. Students must apply separately for "Housing" during the Step 2 phase of the application. ​5.2. Residence Rules: Students residing in University accommodation are bound by the Specific Rules for Residences, which prohibit sub-letting, unauthorized guests, and any behavior that disrupts the academic environment. ​6. DECLARATION OF COMMITMENT ​I, the Applicant/Student, hereby acknowledge that I have read and understood the Institutional Rules and Regulations. I accept that my relationship with the Institution is governed by these policies and that the Institution’s decision regarding admission and disciplinary matters is final.
